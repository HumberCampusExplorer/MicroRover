Early Adopter Instructions

  1. Thanks to our generous students (via lost and found) you have the beginnings of a parts kit - if not, contact me.
  2. I will link to a number of draft videos in this workshop guide, in this case it is a video that guides the students to purchase a parts kit at the bookstore.
  3. You also have a partial starter kit of parts for the workshop on May 15th which I hope that you get started on over the next week. Hassan, Leon, Mihyar, Brandeen, Jeremy, Muhammad.
  4. The partial starter kit contains:
    1. One 3.7 V 350 mAh Lithium Ion Polymer 552035 Cell,
    2. One Trinket LiPoly Backpack Charger,
    3. Two BS1806-022 O-rings
    4. Two 3D printed micro servo wheels
    5. Two Micro Continuous Rotation Servos, one of which is packaged with:
    6. Six terminal posts,
    7. One 3V Adafruit Trinket, packaged with:
    8. >30 cm of SN 100C 0.032‚Äù diameter lead free no clean rosin based glow core flux core of 2.5%.
  5. In your student donated parts kit find:
    1. One 100 kΩ resistor
    2. One 100 Ω resistor
    3. A 5 cm length of solid core hookup wire
  6. Please put on your glasses for safety as we will be soldering and using side cutters. To solder these parts:
    1. Take a length of solid core hookup wire (from your student supplied parts kit) and strip two 5 cm lengths of it.
    2. Use pliers to create a bend as shown:
    Solder those along with the resistors onto the provided PCB: Trim the leads and then solder the terminal posts.
  7. Unfortunately we do not have a USB cable - A/MicroB - 3ft in stock for each of you yet. If you have a suitable cable, for say your phone, please use it to upload blink to your trinket to confirm that it works:
    1. Log into a computer in J232.
    2. Start Arduino IDE.
    3. Continue to the next step.
  8. Soldering headers:
    1. Solder the provided headers (use side cutters to cut the headers to the appropriate length making sure not to send to parts flying) onto the trinket.
    2. Solder the provided headers onto the trinket backpack.
  9. Unfortunately we do not have a USB cable - A/MicroB - 3ft in stock for each of you yet. If you have a suitable cable, for say your phone, please use it to upload blink to your trinket to confirm it is undamaged:
    1. Log into a computer in J232.
    2. Start Arduino IDE.
    3. Continue to the next step.
